02 — FinOps Quickstart

The problem it solves

Cloud bills grow in ways that are hard to explain and harder to defend. Reserved instance commitments expire. Unused resources accumulate. Data transfer costs pile up silently. By the time someone notices, the waste has been compounding for months — sometimes years.

What we deliver

A focused two-week sprint that surfaces exactly where your cloud spend is going, identifies the highest-impact waste, and delivers a concrete savings roadmap. We pay particular attention to network costs — data egress, inter-region transfer, NAT gateway, and transit charges — which are consistently the most opaque line items on any cloud bill.

What you walk away with

  • Full cost breakdown by service, team, environment, and cloud
  • Identified waste with estimated annual savings for each item
  • Network cost analysis — the line items most reviews overlook
  • Tagging and cost allocation recommendations
  • A prioritized action plan with quick wins and longer-term commitments

04 — Cloud Security Foundations

The problem it solves

Cloud security debt is quiet until it isn't. Overpermissioned IAM roles, missing guardrails, unclear public access settings, and no policy enforcement layer are common in environments that were built fast. The problem compounds when an enterprise customer sends a security questionnaire — or when something goes wrong.

What we deliver

The structural security layer that makes a cloud environment defensible: IAM architecture, least-privilege access design, service control policies, and organizational guardrails across every cloud you run. This is cloud security architecture, not penetration testing or managed security operations — we build the foundations.

What you walk away with

  • IAM architecture with least-privilege role design and access documentation
  • Service control policies, Azure Policies, or GCP Org Policies enforced at the organizational level
  • Security baselines across compute, storage, networking, and identity
  • A security posture your team can maintain and extend

FAQ

Common questions.

Do you work with a single cloud?
Yes — though multi-cloud environments are where we have the deepest expertise. Whether you're running entirely on AWS or managing workloads across three platforms, we'll work with what you have.
What does a typical engagement look like?
Most client relationships start with a Cloud Health Audit or a FinOps Quickstart — a fixed-price, time-boxed engagement that gives both sides a clear picture of what's needed. Larger engagements follow from there, scoped based on what the audit surfaces.
What happens if scope changes mid-project?
Any change to agreed deliverables requires a signed change request before additional work begins. We define this clearly in the statement of work upfront. It protects both sides — you get a clear price before we proceed, and we don't do unpaid work.
Do you need access to our cloud accounts?
For most engagements, yes. We follow least-privilege access principles and only request the permissions needed for the specific task. We use role-based access — IAM roles, service principals, workload identity — never root or admin credentials. We sign an NDA before any access is granted.
Do you do cloud migrations?
We handle cloud-to-cloud migrations and workload modernization as part of our Cloud Foundation Build engagements. For large-scale lift-and-shift migrations from on-premises environments, we'll be direct about what's in scope for us and refer you to the right specialists for what isn't.

Not sure which service fits your situation?

Send us a note describing your environment and what's causing friction. We'll review it and follow up to schedule a conversation — and we'll be honest about what makes sense, even if that's not us.
